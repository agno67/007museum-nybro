About the Museum

A once-in-a-lifetime
experience

Tucked away in the heart of Nybro, Sweden, the James Bond 007 Museum is an internationally recognized cultural phenomenon — and the most unique Bond destination on earth. Founded in 1965 by a man who would legally become James Bond himself, the museum holds artefacts, vehicles, props, and memorabilia found nowhere else in the world.

Whether you are a lifelong 007 devotee or simply curious, you will leave having experienced something truly extraordinary. Visitors consistently describe it as a highlight of their travels — an intimate, personal encounter with one man's singular obsession spanning more than six decades.

From iconic film vehicles and rare model cars to signed memorabilia and the legendary gondola from the films, every corner of this museum tells a story that no other institution in the world can tell.

Meet the Founder

The Man Behind
the Legend

A life dedicated to the world's most iconic secret agent

Gunnar "James Bond" Schäfer Founder & Director, Est. 1965
"

Some obsessions define a life. For Gunnar Schäfer — officially and legally known as James Bond — his began in 1964 with a single film: Goldfinger. What followed was not merely a hobby, but a six-decade mission to build the world's most comprehensive and personal tribute to 007.

His museum in Nybro, Sweden is internationally recognized as a one-of-a-kind destination — housing rare memorabilia, a real Bond gondola, iconic film-related artefacts, vehicles, and objects found in no other museum on earth. Every piece has a story; Gunnar knows them all.

Sought out as a Bond authority by Swedish national radio — including P4 Kalmar and P4 Kronoberg — Gunnar has been the go-to expert for film releases including Skyfall and Spectre. He has personally visited Bond filming locations across the globe and has met every actor who has portrayed James Bond, including a personal encounter with Sir Roger Moore.

In 2023, Gunnar's remarkable story reached a global audience through his appearance in the documentary The Other Fellow (2023) — a film exploring what it means to share a name with the world's most famous spy.

To visit the museum is to meet Gunnar himself. And that, as countless visitors attest, is the greatest part of all.
